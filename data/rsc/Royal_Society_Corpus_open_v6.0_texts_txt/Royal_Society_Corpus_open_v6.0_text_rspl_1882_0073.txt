The question of the precise degree of the conductivity for heat of the tissues lying between the surface of the brain and the outer surface of the integument is , of course , of the first importance in studying the possible effects on the exterior of the skin of changes of temperature occurring in the superficial layers of the cerebrum ; and the question of the degree of conductivity of brain-tissue itself is of great importance with reference to the extent to which propagation through the cerebral mass of thermal changes occurring in a single point or tract of the brain may take place*
